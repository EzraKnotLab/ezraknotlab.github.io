Thomas Frank 
​

Hey there!

This is a super quick email, in which I want to ask you a question:

If you wish you read more books, what's keeping you from doing that?

If you have a second, reply to this email and let me know.

Hi, honestly my passion for reading comes from time to time, maybe it's just due to my time spent on other things. 
Sometimes I read a lot in a day, and I also read ten pages in a week...I wish I could read more books if my time were permitted. 
1st, I love that cozy and quiet atmosphere. 
Reading around this kind of environment makes me feel connected with nature. 
2nd, certain themed books also interest me in exploring more about this cosmos, like "Out of Africa" by Karen Blixen. 
3rd, there's an old saying that goes:  It is better to travel ten thousand miles than to read ten thousand books. 
we call it 读万卷书，行万里路in Chinese.(uh originally it's a Chinese saying). But it was translated in the wrong way. 
Life is a long road to take, and along the way, the more books I read,  the more I feel I'm flawed as a human being. 
4th last but not least, reading satisfies my curiosity, like Malcolm X said 'My alma mater was books, a good library..... could spend the rest of my life reading, just satisfying my curiosity.'

Best regards
Bruno Leopold
