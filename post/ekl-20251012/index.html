Information Hunger: The Urge to Constantly Refresh Social Media

↩️
In today’s hyper-connected world, a new phenomenon has emerged: information hunger. It’s that restless feeling that creeps in when you haven’t checked your social media feeds for a few hours—or even minutes. You find yourself compulsively refreshing apps, chasing the dopamine rush of new videos, fresh posts, or the latest live streams. This insatiable craving for updates stems from our desire to stay in the loop, fueled by the endless stream of content on platforms like X. Whether it’s breaking news, viral clips, or live broadcasts, the fear of missing out (FOMO) drives us to keep scrolling, refreshing, and consuming. But this constant need for new information can leave us overwhelmed, distracted, and even anxious.Why do we do this? Social media is designed to keep us hooked, with algorithms curating content to spark curiosity and engagement. Each refresh promises something novel, creating a cycle that’s hard to break. To manage this information hunger, try setting intentional boundaries—like limiting screen time or curating your feed to prioritize meaningful content. After all, staying informed doesn’t have to mean drowning in a sea of updates.

Just Do It: Let Iteration Pave the Optimal Path

↩️
In a world obsessed with perfection, the simplest advice is often the most powerful: just do it. Don’t wait for the perfect plan or the ideal moment—start where you are, with what you have. Action breeds clarity, and through relentless iteration, the best path naturally emerges.Every step forward, no matter how small, is a chance to learn, adapt, and refine. Like a sculptor chiseling away at stone, each iteration sharpens your vision and hones your approach. The beauty of this mindset is its freedom: you don’t need to know the endgame. Trust the process, embrace the mess, and let the optimal path evolve organically through persistent effort.So, take the leap. Start that project, post that content, or chase that idea. With every tweak and pivot, you’re not just moving—you’re evolving toward something better.

提着灯笼赶路+理论

↩️

不需要把每个步骤和细节都考虑清楚，也不用提前预想所有的的困难和麻烦、解决方案。现实的人生更像在黑暗中摸索一一没有办法提前看清所有的坑，最好的方法就是拿着灯笼小心往前走。虽然灯光能够照到的距离是有限的，但是每走一步，下一段就亮了。

    • 定义创意很困难。柏林会议上的一致意见是，科学中的创意是 “新颖”且“值得追求” 的事物的交集，正如科学哲学家 茱莉亚·桑切斯-多拉多（Julia Sánchez-Dorado） 在 2023 年的一项研究中所理论化的那样。
    • 计算机科学家 笠原俊一（Shunichi Kasahara） 认为，研究中的创意是“从经验中观察、迭代和提取有意义的洞察力的能力”。
    • 无论定义如何，个人经历塑造创意这一点得到了广泛认同。 大的建议是阅读其他领域的资料；去听其他领域的讲座。”
    • 亚奈和莱歇尔认为，将科学的创意方面与其余部分区分开来看待是有益的，这种二分法被昵称为昼夜科学（night-and-day science）。这个概念最初由法国生物学家和诺贝尔奖获得者弗朗索瓦·雅各布（François Jacob）提出。亚奈说：“昼科学是执行部分。你有了想法，为了检验它，你进行对照实验。”他解释说：“夜科学是创意的世界，是想法的世界。在夜科学中，我们不谈论实验的具体细节，而是使用比喻和拟人化的语言。我们提倡首先要认识到存在一个夜科学世界。一旦你认识到这一点，你就会意识到我们在这个世界中也需要训练。”

    • Casablanca：这个摩洛哥城市的名字原本来自西班牙语/葡萄牙语“Casa Blanca”。1515年，葡萄牙探险家将当地一个白色的灯塔（或白色房屋）命名为“Casa Branca”（葡萄牙语），后来西班牙语版本“Casablanca”流行开来。原本地名是柏柏尔语的“Anfa”，但这个“白房子”昵称保留至今。

    • Casa Blanca：美国白宫的西班牙语直译（这个比较简单，casa家 House ， Blanca 白色的 形容词一般后置），就是“白色的房子”。因为白宫的建筑是白色的砂岩刷白漆。
