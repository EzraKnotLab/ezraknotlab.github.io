"Philly shell" is a defensive boxing stance or strategy that is associated with fighters from Philadelphia, hence the name. This defensive technique involves holding the lead hand low and close to the body, positioning the shoulder to protect the chin, and using head movement to slip and dodge punches. The rear hand is typically held higher to provide additional protection. Fighters utilizing the Philly shell rely on quick reflexes, upper body movement, and counter-punching to evade attacks while creating opportunities to launch their own offensive moves. It's a distinctive and often effective defensive style that has been employed by various successful boxers, contributing to its recognition in the world of combat sports.
